Some stuff about integration by parts.

Recall the product rule for differentiation which says that if \(u\) and \(v\) are differentiable functions of \(x\) then \[\begin{equation*} (uv)' = uv' + vu', \end{equation*}\] where the notation \(u'\) is used for derivative of \(u\) with respect to \(x\). Integration of both sides of this equation with respect to \(\mathrm d x\) and in view of the fact that the differentials of \(u\) and \(v\) are \[\begin{equation*} \mathrm d u = u' \; \mathrm d x \qquad\text{ and } \qquad \mathrm d v = v' \; \mathrm d x \end{equation*}\] respectively, we obtain \[\begin{equation*} \begin{split} uv & = \int (uv)'\; \mathrm d x\\ & = \int u\; v' \mathrm d x + \int v\; u'\mathrm d x\\ & = \int u\; \mathrm d v + \int v \; \mathrm d u \end{split} \end{equation*}\] Upon rearranging we obtain \[\begin{equation*} \int u \; \mathrm d v = uv - \int v\; \mathrm d u \end{equation*}\]
